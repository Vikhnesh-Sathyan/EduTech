Fundamentals of Music Theory

This course, provided by The University of Edinburgh, is designed for aspiring musicians, composers, and anyone who wants to deepen their understanding of music. Whether you're a beginner or already familiar with music, this course will provide you with a comprehensive understanding of the foundational elements of music theory.

What You Will Learn

The course covers essential topics that will help you understand how music works, enabling you to read, write, and compose your own pieces.

Click on the topics below to learn more:

Introduction to Music Theory

Music theory forms the foundation for all musical understanding. In this section, you'll learn about the core elements of music, including rhythm, melody, harmony, and the basics of musical structure.

We will discuss how these elements come together to form the building blocks of songs, symphonies, and everything in between. You will also explore historical perspectives on how music theory developed over time, evolving into what it is today.

Musical Notation

Understanding musical notation is essential for reading and writing music. In this topic, we will cover the various symbols and signs used in music notation, such as notes, rests, time signatures, and key signatures.

Additionally, you'll gain hands-on experience with different types of notations used in various music genres such as classical, jazz, and modern pop.

Scales and Intervals

Scales and intervals are fundamental to understanding the relationships between notes in music. You will learn about major and minor scales, how they are constructed, and the role intervals play in creating melodies and harmonies.

In addition to learning theory, you will practice using scales and intervals to compose original pieces and to analyze famous works from various composers.

Chords and Harmony

Chords are created by combining multiple notes, and harmony occurs when these chords support the melody. This topic will teach you how to construct chords, understand chord progressions, and analyze harmonic structures.

Through exercises and case studies, you'll discover how harmony contributes to the emotional depth of music in classical, film scores, and contemporary music.

Practical Exercises

In this section, you'll engage in practical exercises designed to reinforce the theoretical concepts you've learned. These exercises include composing simple melodies, harmonizing them, and analyzing music to understand how the theory is applied in real-world musical settings.

Additional exercises will guide you through creating variations of melodies, experimenting with different harmonic structures, and using scales in innovative ways.

Course Objectives

By the end of the course, you will:

  • Be able to read and write musical notation fluently.
  • Understand key concepts such as time signatures, scales, intervals, and chords.
  • Recognize common harmonic structures in different types of music.
  • Have the ability to compose and analyze simple pieces of music.
